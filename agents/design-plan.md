# Design Plan - Skeleton Crew (Round 2: Post-Feedback)

## Status: AWAITING OPERATOR CONVERSATION

The operator has not signed off on any design assets. The existing logo variants (v1-v3) were produced without operator approval. This plan proposes a fresh direction based on operator feedback and is presented for discussion before any production work begins.

---

## What Changed Since Round 1

1. **Font change:** H1/hero font is now **Permanent Marker** (replacing Lacquer). This affects the logo because the brief says logo text should have a "graffiti-influenced style" - and Permanent Marker has a different character to Lacquer. Permanent Marker is bolder, more marker-pen, more street. Lacquer was more brush-painted and loose. The logo lettering should now sit comfortably alongside Permanent Marker headlines without feeling like a different universe.

2. **Operator feedback:** "Too formulaic." The site needs to feel like "a subversive punk site that's disrupting the norm." The existing logos are competent but safe - they look like a nice skeleton icon with clean text underneath. They don't have the raw, confrontational energy of a Palace lookbook or a Thrasher cover.

3. **Skate reference recalibration:** Element, Palace, Supreme, Thrasher - these brands are not polished. Their logos have attitude. The current Skeleton Crew logos feel more like "design agency that likes skate culture" than "skate brand that happens to build websites." The gap is in the aggression and imperfection.

---

## Critique of Existing Logo Variants

### What works
- The skull character with the neon green eye dot is a strong brand device - keep it
- Text-as-paths approach is correct (no font dependencies)
- Colour palette (#f5f5f5, #00FF41 on #0a0a0a) is strong
- The icon-only skull mark is solid for favicon use

### What does not work
- **Too symmetrical.** Every variant is centred, balanced, orderly. Punk is not orderly. The compositions feel like they were designed by committee, not scrawled on a wall at 2am.
- **Text is too clean.** The letterforms are described as "hand-painted" but they read as a geometric sans-serif with minor wobble. There is no grit, no drip, no spray, no weight variation. They look like a clean font, not like hand-lettering.
- **The skeleton is too friendly.** The current skull is a nice, symmetrical, well-proportioned cartoon skull. It needs more character - an asymmetric grin, a cracked tooth, something that makes it look like it has been through something. Think Powell Peralta's Ripper, not a Halloween decoration.
- **No compositional tension.** Everything sits neatly in its box. The stacked lockup is tidy. The inline version is tidy. The tag version is tidy. None of them make you feel anything. A subversive brand needs visual tension - something slightly off, slightly uncomfortable, slightly anarchic.
- **No texture or grit.** Pure clean vectors. The brief references spray paint, skateboard decks, wheat-paste posters. These surfaces have texture. The logo should feel like it has been somewhere - stickered on a lamppost, stamped on grip tape, spray-painted through a stencil.

## Proposed Direction: 3 New Variants

All variants retain the skull-with-green-eye as the core mark. All text is drawn as SVG paths (no fonts). All use only #f5f5f5, #00FF41, and #0a0a0a.

### Variant A: "Stencil Bomb" (lead candidate)

**Concept:** A stencil-cut logo that looks like it was sprayed onto a concrete wall. The skull sits inside a rough rectangular frame (like a stencil border) with "SKELETON CREW" text below it, also stencilled. The frame is not a clean rectangle - it has rough, torn edges suggesting cardboard or acetate that has been cut and reused.

**Skull treatment:** Front-facing, but asymmetric. The grin is wider on one side. One eye socket is slightly larger than the other. The neon green dot sits in the smaller eye - making it feel like a wink or a knowing look. Teeth are uneven - one is cracked or missing. The overall impression: this skull has personality. It is not menacing, it is mischievous.

**Text treatment:** Chunky stencil-cut capitals. The letterforms have the characteristic stencil bridges (gaps in letters like A, B, D, O, R where the stencil needs to stay connected). This is a deliberate aesthetic choice - it references actual stencil art and gives the text a raw, street quality. Letters are heavy, tight-spaced, and slightly irregular in baseline. Some letters may have minor spray bleed at their edges (achieved with slightly rough path edges, not filters).

**Composition:** The skull and frame sit above the text, but the frame is angled 2-3 degrees off-horizontal - a subtle tilt that breaks the formality. The text below is straight, creating tension between the two elements.

**Why this direction:** Stencil art is the visual language of street protest, DIY culture, and skateboarding. It communicates "we made this ourselves" - which is exactly Skeleton Crew's positioning against the Wix/Squarespace template factories. The stencil bridges also give the text a unique quality that no font replicates.

### Variant B: "Deck Graphic"

**Concept:** The logo as it would appear on the base of a skateboard deck. The skull is the dominant element, rendered larger and more aggressively. "SKELETON" arcs above the skull, "CREW" arcs below it - like a classic skate brand deck graphic or a biker patch. The overall shape is roughly oval/badge-like but with deliberately broken edges.

**Skull treatment:** More detailed than Variant A. The skull includes the upper torso (collar bones, ribs) but rendered with heavy, confident lines that vary in weight - thick outlines with thinner interior detail. The expression is more intense - wider grin, deeper eye sockets. The neon green eye dot is larger and has a slight radial gradient effect (achieved with concentric circles, not raster gradients) suggesting glow. A diagonal crack runs across the cranium - the skull has been through it.

**Text treatment:** Bold, condensed, uppercase. The arcing text follows a curve around the skull. The letterforms are chunkier and more aggressive than Round 1 - they reference vintage skate deck typography from the 80s/90s. Slightly condensed vertically, expanded horizontally. Not stencilled - these are bold marker-style letters with rough edges and weight variation as if drawn with a fat Sharpie.

**Composition:** Centred but with internal asymmetry - the crack in the skull, the green eye on one side, slight variations in the arc spacing. The overall silhouette is badge-shaped, which works well for social media avatars and merchandise.

**Why this direction:** This is the most "skate" of the three options. It references the golden era of deck graphics directly. The badge shape is highly versatile for round/square contexts (social avatars, stickers, patches). It is bolder and more confrontational than Round 1.

### Variant C: "Wheat Paste"

**Concept:** The logo as a wheat-paste poster slapped on a wall. The skull overlaps the text aggressively - it is not neatly separated. "SKELETON" and "CREW" are stacked in two massive, heavy lines with the skull punching through from behind, partially obscuring some letters. The effect is layered, like posters pasted over each other on a construction hoarding.

**Skull treatment:** Mid-detail. Rendered entirely in neon green (#00FF41) as a contrasting layer against the off-white text. The skull peeks through from behind/between letters, creating a sense of depth. Only the upper portion is visible (cranium, eyes, nose) - the jaw and teeth are obscured by the text. The green skull behind white text creates a striking two-tone effect.

**Text treatment:** The heaviest text of all three variants. Extremely bold, tight tracking, almost touching. The letterforms are blocky and angular - imagine letters cut from plywood with a jigsaw. Rough, splintery edges. "SKELETON" and "CREW" are stacked with minimal line spacing so they almost merge into a single block of text. Some letters have notches or chips cut out of them.

**Composition:** The text dominates. The skull is a background element that creates visual intrigue - you notice it second. The overall shape is a dense rectangular block. This variant works at small sizes because the text is the primary element, and it works at large sizes because the skull detail rewards closer inspection.

**Why this direction:** Wheat-pasting is guerrilla marketing - cheap, fast, everywhere. It is the visual language of bands, underground clubs, and subcultures. This variant feels the most "underground" and the most like actual street art. The layered composition is more visually complex and interesting than a clean lockup.

## Skull Icon / Favicon

The icon-only skull mark needs the same treatment as whichever variant is approved. The current skull icon is too clean and symmetrical. The updated skull should have:

- Asymmetric grin (wider on one side)
- One cracked or missing tooth
- Slightly uneven eye sockets
- The neon green dot in the left eye (brand signature - this stays)
- Heavier, rougher line work with subtle wobble
- Enough simplification to remain legible at 16x16

The favicon SVG retains its dark background with rounded corners.

---

## How the Logo Relates to Permanent Marker

Permanent Marker (the new H1 font) has these characteristics:
- Thick, confident strokes with natural weight variation
- Slightly bouncing baseline
- Informal but highly legible
- Feels like someone wrote it quickly with a fat marker on a whiteboard

The logo text should complement this without copying it. The logo letterforms should be heavier, tighter-spaced, and more constructed than Permanent Marker's natural handwriting feel. Think: Permanent Marker is how you write fast, the logo is how you write when you are making a statement. Same energy family, different intensity.

## Questions for the Operator

Before I produce anything, I want your input on these:

1. **Which variant direction appeals most?** Stencil Bomb (raw, street), Deck Graphic (classic skate badge), or Wheat Paste (layered, underground)? Or a combination of elements from multiple directions?

2. **Skull personality:** I have proposed asymmetric grin, cracked tooth, uneven eyes. How far do you want to push the imperfection? Should the skull feel "cheeky and confident" or "battle-scarred and gnarly"?

3. **Stencil bridges (Variant A):** The gaps in letters like O, A, R that real stencils require - do you like this as a deliberate aesthetic, or does it feel like a gimmick?

4. **Green skull (Variant C):** The idea of the skull being entirely neon green as a background layer behind white text - does that feel right, or should the skull always be off-white with just the green eye dot?

5. **How rough is too rough?** The feedback says "subversive punk" but the site still needs to communicate "we are professionals who will build you a great website." Where is the line for you? Should the logo feel like it belongs in a gallery show about street art (curated roughness) or like it was actually found on a wall (genuine roughness)?

6. **Any strong feelings about the existing skull?** Is there anything about the current skull character you want to keep, or should I treat it as a complete restart?

## Technical Constraints (Unchanged)

- All SVGs: viewBox-based, no fixed width/height
- All text: converted to paths, zero font dependencies
- No embedded raster images
- No filters requiring raster rendering (no feGaussianBlur, feDropShadow)
- Colours: only #0a0a0a, #00FF41, #f5f5f5
- Accessible `<title>` element in each SVG
- Clean path data, integer or max 2 decimal precision
- Skull mark legible at 16x16px
